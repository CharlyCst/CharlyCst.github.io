Research Statement

Software is going through a trust crisis. Our society critically relies on software, from the apps on our smartphones to the systems that power our businesses and governments, but software itself is getting out of control. Nowadays, the simplest applications embed hundreds of dependencies written by thousands of independent developers, while our operating systems, kernels, and hypervisors are exposing an ever-growing attack surface that results in a never-ending flow of critical vulnerabilities.

Our systems, whose fundamental design originates from the ’70s, are unable to face the complexity of the modern world: trust in the all-mighty privileged code is eroded, compute is moving to the public cloud, and our applications are exponentially growing in size. The abstractions baked in our systems, processes and virtual machines, can no longer keep up with the increasingly complex trust relationships between software components.

The way we build and compose software is in desperate need for an update. My research focus on developing new isolation abstractions and mechanisms to power our systems for the next 50 years. I rethink how isolation should work, from programming languages and compilers all the way down to operating systems and hypervisors.

Semantic Operating Systems 2023

The design of operating systems in wide use today is rooted in systems originally developed in the seventies, and hence inherits design decisions constrained by the available tooling of that time. In particular, todayâ€™s operating systems lack understanding of programs, leading to the need for costly isolation, unstructured interfaces between software components, and lack of flexibility of execution. Yet, programming language design and compiler toolchains made stunning progress during the last two decades and opened the door to radically different designs for a new generation of operating systems. In fact, such designs already emerge in both research and widely used legacy systems, achieving better performance with increased reliability.

Stateful Confidential Computing 2021-2022

Confidential computing offers very few options for data management, in practice limited to the generation of keys private to the enclave. This has the unfortunate consequence of preventing the transparent software upgrade of stateful services, such as confidential databases, when the service operator cannot be trusted. Indeed, data migration could be trivially abused by a malicious operator to either leak data or inject corrupt data into the restarted enclave.

We propose attestable software versioning to ensure the trust-worthy software migration of stateful enclaves in the context of an untrusted service operator.
